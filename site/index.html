The setup

AI is the largest energy story of our lifetime

By 2030, data centers will use more electricity than Japan (IEA Energy and AI, April 2025). Global power demand grows near 4% per year through 2027. Every major grid — China, US, Europe, India — is repricing electricity in real time.

Demand is one side. Supply is the other — and supply runs through chokepoints. Hormuz, Russia–Ukraine, Red Sea, Taiwan, climate volatility — geopolitics rewires energy flows in days.

The gap

Forecasts are stuck in the past

Today's energy intelligence comes from a handful of consulting firms. One firm, one model, one opinion — sold for hundreds of thousands of dollars per seat, updated quarterly. Meanwhile, the actual market moves every five minutes.

Power traders, utilities, and data-center operators fly blind in the most volatile energy environment in 50 years.

The idea

Flux is a marketplace for forecasts

Anyone submits

Quants. Researchers. Universities.

Fork the reference model. Improve it. Submit. Bittensor emissions reward the best automatically.
Reality decides

Public grid data resolves everything.

ERCOT publishes the realized price. Either your forecast matched, or it didn't. No DAO. No LLM judge. No dispute.
Customers buy the best

Traders. Utilities. Data centers.

Three tiers from $5k to $100k+ per month. Enterprise API served by validators.
Open competition

Many models beat one model.

Bloomberg and Modo sell single proprietary outputs. Flux scores hundreds against reality every 72 minutes.
The mechanism

How it works

Every ~72 minutes (one Bittensor tempo):

StepWhat happens
1Miners submit sealed probabilistic forecasts across 5min · 1h · 24h · 1-week horizons
2Validators pull realized prices from the grid's public API
3Score each miner: 0.5·Skill + 0.3·Calibration + 0.2·Breadth
4Commit-reveal weights on-chain (5-tempo delay defeats copiers)
5Serve the best forecasts via enterprise API

Eight named attacks. Eight named defenses. Each maps to one specific hyperparameter or capital commitment — no human dependency anywhere in the scoring loop.
