The 8-minute pitch

Time-coded script per slide. Read the spoken text; check the italicized cues for body language and emphasis.

Pacing budget

16 slides · 480 seconds total · ~30s average · weight hook slides (1–5) heavier, mechanism slides (8–13) lighter. Aim to finish at 7:45 leaving 15s for the last beat to land.

0:00–0:251Title25s

"Hi. I'm Edward. I'm here to show you Flux — a marketplace for energy forecasts, built on Bittensor."

"In 8 minutes you'll know exactly what we're building, who's paying for it, and why nobody else can build it the way we do."

Stand still. Slow down. The room should hear "marketplace · energy forecasts · Bittensor" — that's the one-line memory.

0:25–1:252AI is the largest energy story of our lifetime60s

"By 2030, data centers will use more electricity than Japan. Look at this chart — the IEA's projection. We're going from 415 terawatt-hours in 2024 to 945 by 2030. That's doubling in five years."

"And it's not just AI. Global power demand is growing close to 4% every year through 2027. EVs, electrified industry, cooling load — all compounding at once."

Point at the 2030 bar. Pause. Let the chart do the work. The audience already believes AI is huge — you're just connecting it to power.

1:25–2:103Geopolitics rewires the grid45s

"That's the demand side. Supply is the other half — and supply runs through chokepoints."

"Hormuz. Russia–Ukraine. Red Sea. Taiwan. Climate volatility. Every one of these can reshape global power markets in days."

"Demand is exploding. Supply is fragile. Prices move in minutes."

Look up from the slide. Say "in minutes" with weight. This is where the urgency lives.

2:10–2:404Forecasts are stuck in the past30s

"Yet today's energy intelligence comes from a handful of consulting firms. One firm, one model, one opinion. PDFs. Quarterly. $300k a seat."

"The market moves every 5 minutes. Traders are flying blind."

Snap-cadence. Three short stabs: "one firm — one model — one opinion." Hand gesture: one finger, one finger, one finger.

2:40–3:255That's what Flux is45s

"Flux is a marketplace for energy forecasts."

"Anyone can submit a forecast — quants, researchers, universities. Public grid data resolves who was right, automatically. Customers buy the top-ranked outputs. The network rewards winners on-chain."

"No single vendor. No proprietary black box. No quarterly delay."

Read the four cards left-to-right, top-to-bottom. Quick, rhythmic. The audience should feel the symmetry.

"Three customer tiers. Crypto-quant funds — $60k a year. Trading desks — $300k. Enterprise utilities and data center operators — $1.2 million plus."

"Three-year ARR target: $15 to $30 million across validators."

"Our wedge is crypto-native energy desks. They already trust Bittensor. They already trade ERCOT. They're underserved by Bloomberg and Modo. That wedge alone funds the first 18 months."

This is the slide investors care about. Slow down on the dollar numbers. Make eye contact.

4:10–4:407Why this works on Bittensor30s

"Every decentralized AI subnet struggles with one question: who decides who's right?"

"Flux's answer is: the grid does. ERCOT publishes the realized price. Your forecast matched, or it didn't. Public data. Deterministic. Final."

This is the intellectual core of the pitch. Say it slowly. Bittensor judges will lock onto this.

Don't dwell. The audience trusts you have a model.

Likely Q&A · prep these answers

Q: How do you actually compete with Modo when they have $30M in the bank?

A: We're not competing for the same customer. Modo sells project-finance bankability — quarterly. We sell trading-floor operational forecasts — real-time. Adjacent markets. If Modo expands into real-time within 24 months, our network effect from open competition is the moat — they can't match the diversity of hundreds of independent quants with one in-house team.

Q: What happens if Bittensor's economics change again?

A: We've designed for Taoflow's flow-based emission risk. $1M owner pre-fund plus 20% API revenue redirect during low-emission periods. Stickier with 6-month staker incentives. Subnet survives a quarter of negative flow without miner exodus.

Q: Why would energy traders trust a Bittensor subnet?

A: They won't, day one. Our first 20 customers are crypto-native energy desks — they already trust both sides. That funds 18 months. By month 24, we have a track record traders can audit on-chain.

Q: What if miners can't beat the naive baseline?

A: Then the mechanism is wrong and we kill it. This is one of our three six-month gate tests. We test before launch on 30 days of ERCOT data. If competitive miners can't beat yesterday+seasonal by 20%, we don't ship.

Q: Why ERCOT first and not CAISO or PJM?

A: Three reasons. Highest LMP volatility in the US, so biggest forecast value. Fully open data API, so validators score for free. And it's the grid with the biggest AI-data-center repricing story — the narrative that closes meetings.

Q: How big can this actually get?

A: Realistically, $50–150M enterprise value over 5 years. Not a unicorn play. The ceiling is real — 256 UID cap, single-vertical, Modo will eventually expand. But it's a real $15–30M ARR business that compounds.
