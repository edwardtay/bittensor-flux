Likely Q&A

How do you compete with Modo when they have 30 million dollars in the bank?

We are not competing for the same customer. Modo sells project-finance bankability, quarterly. We sell trading-floor operational forecasts, real-time. Adjacent markets. If Modo expands into real-time within 24 months, our network effect from open competition is the moat. They cannot match the diversity of hundreds of independent quants with one in-house team.

What happens if Bittensor's economics change again?

We have designed for Taoflow's flow-based emission risk. 1 million dollar owner pre-fund plus 20 percent API revenue redirect during low-emission periods. Stickier with 6-month staker incentives. The subnet survives a quarter of negative flow without miner exodus.

Why would energy traders trust a Bittensor subnet?

They will not, day one. Our first 20 customers are crypto-native energy desks. They already trust both sides. That funds 18 months. By month 24, we have a track record traders can audit on-chain.

What if miners cannot beat the naive baseline?

Then the mechanism is wrong and we kill it. This is one of our three six-month gate tests. We test before launch on 30 days of ERCOT data. If competitive miners cannot beat yesterday plus seasonal by 20 percent, we do not ship.

Why ERCOT first and not CAISO or PJM?

Three reasons. Highest LMP volatility in the United States, so biggest forecast value. Fully open data API, so validators score for free. And it is the grid with the biggest AI-data-center repricing story. The narrative that closes meetings.

How big can this actually get?

Realistically, 50 to 150 million dollars enterprise value over 5 years. Not a unicorn play. The ceiling is real. 256 UID cap, single vertical, Modo will eventually expand. But it is a real 15 to 30 million ARR business that compounds.
